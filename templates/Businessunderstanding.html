CRISP-ML // Phase 1

Business Understanding

Strategic analysis of climate vulnerability challenges in Colombian municipalities and definition of machine learning objectives.

Project Domain

Climate Risk Analytics

Scope

Colombian Municipalities

Project Goal

Risk Classification

Problem Context

The Challenge: Climate change has intensified environmental emergencies throughout Colombia. Floods, landslides, heat waves, and droughts increasingly affect vulnerable municipalities, generating economic losses, infrastructure damage, and social instability.

The Need: Governmental and environmental organizations require analytical tools capable of identifying municipalities with high climate vulnerability in order to optimize prevention plans and resource allocation strategies.

The Proposal: This project proposes the development of a Machine Learning framework capable of automatically classifying municipalities according to historical disaster exposure and climatic behavior patterns.

Business Objectives

01
Automatic Vulnerability Classification: Develop a scalable ML-based system capable of categorizing municipalities into climate vulnerability levels.
02
Climate Pattern Detection: Identify relationships between air temperature anomalies and environmental emergency occurrences.
03
Decision Support: Provide actionable insights to environmental institutions and territorial planning entities.

Expected Impact

The proposed system may contribute to improving environmental risk monitoring and prevention mechanisms across Colombian territories.

By leveraging historical disaster records and climate information, public organizations could prioritize high-risk municipalities more efficiently and reduce response times during emergency situations.

Key Analytical Questions

  • Which municipalities present the highest climate vulnerability indicators?
  • Can climate variables explain historical emergency behavior?
  • Which meteorological variables have the highest predictive relevance?
  • Can municipalities be grouped according to similar environmental risk profiles?

Feasibility Analysis

Technical

Python ecosystem compatibility using Pandas, Scikit-Learn, GeoPandas, and Flask.

Data

Availability of open governmental datasets through Colombian public data repositories.

Operational

Low deployment costs and accessible infrastructure for experimentation and visualization.

Risks & Limitations

Some municipalities may contain incomplete climate records or inconsistent disaster reporting frequencies.

Spatial mismatches between meteorological stations and municipal boundaries could affect model precision and require preprocessing adjustments.

Phase Conclusion: The Business Understanding phase established a clear alignment between climate risk management requirements and machine learning opportunities. The identified objectives, analytical questions, and technical feasibility validate the continuation toward data exploration and preprocessing stages.
