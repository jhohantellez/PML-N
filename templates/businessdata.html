CRISP-ML // Phase 1

Automatic Classification of Municipalities According to Climate Vulnerability

Problem definition, business objectives, predictive analytics framework, and technical feasibility for Colombian territories.

Data Sources

2 Datasets (UNGRD + Ideam)

Territorial Scope

Colombian Municipalities

Feasibility

High (Open Data)

Project Context

The Challenge: Climate change has intensified environmental emergencies across Colombia, triggering frequent floods, landslides, and severe droughts. These recurring events impose heavy social and economic burdens, requiring advanced analytical frameworks to assess territorial vulnerability effectively.

The Proposal: Deploy Machine Learning models to automatically classify and rank municipalities based on risk levels. This approach crosses historical disaster records with air temperature time-series data to identify high-exposure patterns.

Business Objectives

01
Automatic Classification: Build a scalable Machine Learning pipeline capable of categorizing Colombian municipalities into distinct climate vulnerability tiers.
02
Climate Feature Correlation: Uncover hidden relationships and mathematical dependencies between air temperature anomalies and emergency frequency.
03
Data-Driven Decisions: Deliver actionable insights and risk profiles to support public environmental entities and regional resource allocation.

Available Datasets Evaluation

Historical UNGRD Dataset
Official historical records of environmental emergencies and disaster events reported per municipality, including timestamps, geographical tags, categories, and damage estimates.
Air Temperature Dataset
Continuous meteorological time-series records captured by environmental monitoring stations deployed across the national territory.

Quality Assurance Note: Spatial resolution mismatches and structural missing values between meteorological stations and municipal boundaries will require comprehensive preprocessing, imputation, and geospatial mapping strategies.

Key Analytical Questions

  • Which municipalities exhibit the highest historical climate vulnerability indices?
  • Is there a statistically significant temporal lag between extreme temperature peaks and emergency surges?
  • Which specific meteorological features carry the highest predictive weight in vulnerability scoring?
  • Can unsupervised clustering identify territorial patterns sharing identical hazard baselines?

Project Feasibility Analysis

Technical

Standard open-source Data Science ecosystem: Python (Pandas, Scikit-Learn, PyOD, GeoPandas).

Data

Reliable and unrestricted access via official Colombian Open Data platforms (Datos Abiertos Colombia).

Risks

Irregular spatial distribution of meteorological sensors versus strict municipal political boundaries.

Phase Conclusion & Sign-off: The Business Understanding phase successfully establishes a coherent alignment between environmental management requirements and machine learning capabilities. By contextualizing the UNGRD disaster records alongside temperature fluctuations, a clear framework for automated risk ranking has been validated. Backed by solid open-data access and technical feasibility, the project is officially approved to transition into Phase 2: Data Understanding & Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
