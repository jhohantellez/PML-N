Dataset Description

UNGRD Historical Records
Historical environmental emergency records including floods, landslides, droughts, dates, municipalities, event categories, and reported impacts.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Initial exploratory analysis identified significant variability in disaster frequencies across municipalities.

Temperature distributions revealed seasonal fluctuations and several outlier observations associated with extreme climate conditions.

Preliminary correlations suggest possible relationships between increasing temperature anomalies and higher environmental emergency occurrence rates.

Statistical Summary

Numerical variables such as temperature averages and emergency counts were analyzed using descriptive statistics including mean, standard deviation, minimum, and maximum values.

Categorical variables including municipality names and disaster types were evaluated through frequency analysis and distribution inspection.

Core Feature Schema (UNGRD)

Snapshot of the primary variables identified directly from the UNGRD historical registry that will serve as inputs for the vulnerability modeling stage.

Feature Name Data Type Source Description
Date Datetime UNGRD Timestamp of the emergency occurrence (parsed from string, e.g., '2019 Jan 01').
Department Categorical UNGRD Macro-regional state level, useful for higher-level spatial clustering.
Divipola Categorical UNGRD Unique national municipal identification code. Used as the primary key for spatial merging.
Event Categorical UNGRD Target classification of the environmental disaster (e.g., INCENDIO, INUNDACION).
Fatalities Numerical (Int) UNGRD Number of fatalities. Critical weight metric for calculating target hazard severity.
People Numerical (Int) UNGRD Total count of individuals directly affected by the specific emergency event.
Families Numerical (Int) UNGRD Count of affected family units, representing demographic disruption.
Destroyed homes Numerical (Int) UNGRD Infrastructure impact metric counting total destroyed homes.
Damaged homes Numerical (Int) UNGRD Count of partially damaged homes, indicating spread of the hazard's impact.
Hectareas Numerical (Float) UNGRD Environmental damage metric measuring affected land area (critical for wildfires).

Data Quality Evaluation

  • Missing values detected in meteorological station measurements.
  • Temporal inconsistencies identified across historical emergency records.
  • Several municipalities contain incomplete climate observations.
  • Potential duplicate entries require validation and cleaning.

Missing Values & Limitations

Null Values

Climate datasets contain structural gaps caused by unavailable station measurements.

Spatial Coverage

Some municipalities do not contain nearby meteorological monitoring stations.

Consistency

Historical reporting standards may vary depending on region and time period.

Correlation Analysis

Correlation analysis was performed to evaluate relationships between climatic variables and emergency occurrence rates.

Preliminary findings indicate that temperature anomalies may contribute to increasing environmental instability in specific regions.
