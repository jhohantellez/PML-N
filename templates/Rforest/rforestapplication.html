Model Summary

The Random Forest classifier demonstrated strong predictive capability for multiclass climate vulnerability analysis.

Oversampling techniques improved Medium-class representation, increasing recall and reducing imbalance-related bias.

The model successfully captured nonlinear relationships between disaster events, seasonality patterns and municipal risk indicators.

Confusion Matrix

The confusion matrix highlights strong classification performance for Low and High vulnerability levels while Medium presents moderate overlap due to similar climate and disaster-related characteristics.

Interpretation

  • Most municipalities were correctly classified.
  • False positives mainly occurred between Medium and neighboring classes.
  • Oversampling improved Medium class recall significantly.
  • Strong diagonal concentration confirms robust prediction behavior.

Feature Importance

EVENT type was identified as the dominant predictive variable, followed by MONTH and MUNICIPALITY_EVENT_COUNT, demonstrating the importance of climate seasonality and disaster frequency.

Key Findings

  • EVENT variable contributed approximately 43% importance.
  • Temporal variables revealed seasonal climate vulnerability patterns.
  • Municipality event frequency strongly influenced classification.
  • The model effectively captured nonlinear environmental interactions.

Performance Analysis

The Low vulnerability class achieved the highest predictive consistency, demonstrating strong model confidence and stable classification behavior. The Medium class remains the most challenging category due to overlapping environmental characteristics and lower sample representation.

Despite this limitation, the application of bootstrap oversampling significantly improved recall performance for Medium vulnerability municipalities, reducing class imbalance and increasing overall model robustness.

The classifier also showed excellent separation capability across all categories, as confirmed by ROC AUC scores above 0.90, indicating strong generalization performance for multiclass climate vulnerability prediction.

Final Conclusion: The Random Forest classifier achieved strong generalization capability with 82.83% overall accuracy and high multiclass discrimination power. Feature importance analysis confirmed that disaster event type and temporal climate patterns are the strongest predictors of municipality vulnerability levels across Colombia.
