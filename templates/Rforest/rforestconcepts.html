Supervised Learning

Random Forest Classifier

An ensemble method that trains hundreds of decision trees on random data subsets and combines their votes to classify municipalities by climate vulnerability — robust, accurate, and interpretable.

83% Overall Accuracy
100 Trees in Forest
8,641 Training Samples
2,161 Test Samples

How It Works

  • 1

    Bootstrap Sampling

    Multiple random subsets of training data are created with replacement — each one feeds a different tree.

  • 2

    Random Feature Selection

    At each node split, only a random subset of features is evaluated, ensuring diversity across trees.

  • 3

    Independent Tree Growth

    Each tree grows until it reaches max_depth=10 or minimum samples per leaf is met.

  • 4

    Majority Voting

    The final vulnerability class is assigned based on the most frequent prediction across all 100 trees.

After oversampling

The Medium class was originally underrepresented (99 samples vs 1,371 for Low). Bootstrap oversampling raised its sample count to 500, improving precision from 15% to 49%.

Model Summary: The Random Forest classifier was trained on 8,641 samples and evaluated on 2,161, achieving an overall accuracy of 82.83%. The type of disaster event was by far the most predictive feature (43%), followed by temporal indicators like month and year — confirming that climate seasonality plays a role in municipal vulnerability. Class imbalance in the Medium category was addressed through bootstrap oversampling before training.
