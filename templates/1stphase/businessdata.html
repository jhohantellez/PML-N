Phase 1 · Business Understanding

Automatic Classification of Municipalities According to Climate Vulnerability

Definition of the problem, business objectives, key analytical questions, data evaluation, and feasibility analysis for a Machine Learning project focused on climate vulnerability in Colombian municipalities.

CRISP-ML Project Climate vulnerability analysis using environmental and social open datasets from Colombia.
Project Context

Problem Context: Climate change has increased the impact of environmental emergencies in Colombia, affecting municipalities through floods, landslides, droughts, heat waves, and other extreme events. These situations generate social, environmental, and economic consequences that require improved analytical approaches for understanding territorial vulnerability.


This project proposes the use of Machine Learning techniques to classify municipalities according to their climate vulnerability using open environmental datasets related to emergencies and air temperature records.

Business Objectives
01
Develop a Machine Learning approach capable of classifying Colombian municipalities according to their climate vulnerability.
02
Identify possible relationships between environmental emergencies and climate variables such as air temperature.
03
Support environmental analysis and territorial decision-making through data-driven insights and predictive analysis.
Key Questions
01
Which municipalities present higher levels of climate vulnerability according to historical environmental information?
02
Is there a relationship between air temperature variations and environmental emergencies?
03
Can Machine Learning models identify patterns associated with vulnerable municipalities?
04
Which regions or municipalities share similar environmental behavior according to the available datasets?
Data Evaluation
UNGRD Emergencies Dataset
This dataset contains historical records of environmental emergencies and disaster-related events reported in Colombian municipalities. The information includes dates, locations, departments, municipalities, and event categories that may contribute to vulnerability analysis.
Air Temperature Dataset
This dataset provides air temperature measurements collected through environmental monitoring stations located in different regions of Colombia. The data may support the analysis of climate behavior associated with environmental emergencies.

Initial Data Assessment: The selected datasets are publicly available and contain structured tabular information suitable for preprocessing and Machine Learning analysis. However, differences in geographic coverage, temporal consistency, and missing values may require additional cleaning and integration processes during later project phases.

Feasibility Analysis
Technical Feasibility
The project can be developed using Python-based technologies such as Pandas, Scikit-Learn, and data visualization libraries for preprocessing, analysis, and model implementation.
Data Availability
The datasets are accessible through official Colombian open data platforms and provide sufficient environmental information for exploratory analysis and model experimentation.
Potential Limitations
Possible challenges include missing values, inconsistencies between datasets, differences in geographic granularity, and the need for data integration before model training.
Phase Conclusion: During the business understanding phase, the climate vulnerability problem affecting Colombian municipalities was defined together with the project objectives, key analytical questions, initial data evaluation, and feasibility considerations. These elements establish the foundation for the following CRISP-ML phases related to data understanding, preprocessing, exploratory analysis, and Machine Learning model development.
